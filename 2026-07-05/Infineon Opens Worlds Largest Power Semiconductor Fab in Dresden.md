## Abstract

Infineon Technologies has officially opened the world's largest fab for power semiconductors and analog/mixed-signal technologies in Dresden, Germany. The new facility represents a multi-billion euro investment and is a cornerstone of European semiconductor sovereignty under the European Chips Act. The fab will produce power semiconductors including IGBTs, MOSFETs, and SiC devices for automotive, industrial, and renewable energy applications.

---

## Key Points

- **Scale:** World's largest fab dedicated to power semiconductors and analog/mixed-signal technologies
- **Location:** Dresden, Germany — "Silicon Saxony" cluster
- **Investment:** Multi-billion euro project (partially funded under European Chips Act)
- **Products:** IGBTs, MOSFETs, SiC (silicon carbide) power devices, analog/mixed-signal ICs
- **Target Markets:** Automotive (EVs, xEV powertrain), industrial motor drives, renewable energy (solar inverters, wind), data center power
- **Strategic Context:** Key pillar of European Union's semiconductor self-sufficiency strategy — reduces dependence on Asian foundries for critical power semiconductors
- **Technology:** 300mm wafer production with advanced automation; process technologies for high-voltage, high-reliability applications
- **Employment:** Creates thousands of high-skilled jobs in Saxony region

---

## Impact Analysis

**Short-term Impact:**
- Immediate capacity relief for automotive power semiconductor supply chain (still recovering from 2021-2023 shortages)
- Strengthens Europe's position in power electronics manufacturing — a critical sector for EV and energy transition
- Boosts Dresden/Silicon Saxony ecosystem, attracting equipment suppliers and materials companies

**Long-term Significance:**
- European Chips Act delivering tangible results — this is one of the largest fabs funded under the initiative
- 300mm power semiconductor production improves cost structure vs. legacy 200mm lines
- SiC production at scale in Europe reduces dependence on US (Wolfspeed) and Chinese SiC supply chains
- Hostile geopolitical environment makes European power semiconductor sovereignty strategically important
- May trigger competitive investments from US (Texas Instruments, Wolfspeed) and Asia (Rohm, ST)

**Key Observation:**
Power semiconductors are the "forgotten critical component" in the AI/GPU narrative. While advanced logic nodes get headlines, the physical world (EVs, factories, power grids, data centers) runs on power semiconductors. Infineon's Dresden fab is arguably as important for European industrial competitiveness as TSMC's Dresden fab is for advanced logic.
