## Abstract

Oxford Instruments Plasma Technology has enabled ROHM's transition to in-house GaN (gallium nitride) manufacturing with advanced atomic layer etching (ALE) technology on 200mm wafers. The collaboration allows ROHM to bring key GaN manufacturing processes in-house, enhancing quality control, supply chain resilience, and competitiveness in the rapidly growing GaN power semiconductor market driven by EV and AI infrastructure applications.

---

## Key Points

- **Technology:** Atomic Layer Etching (ALE) for GaN-on-Si substrates on 200mm wafers
- **Equipment Partner:** Oxford Instruments Plasma Technology
- **Manufacturing Transition:** ROHM moves to fully integrated, in-house GaN production platform
- **Strategic Rationale:** Enhanced control over technology and quality; strengthened supply capabilities; improved competitiveness
- **Target Applications:** Electric vehicles (EVs) and AI infrastructure (power solutions)
- **People:** Yasushi Hamazawa, Executive Officer, ROHM: "We aim to enhance control over technology and quality, strengthen our supply capabilities, and improve our competitiveness in the rapidly growing GaN market"
- **Industry Context:** Growing demand for high-efficiency power solutions in EV chargers, data center PSUs, and renewable energy systems

---

## Impact Analysis

**Short-term Impact:**
- ROHM joins the growing list of IDMs bringing GaN production in-house (vs. fabless/wafer foundry model)
- 200mm GaN-on-Si capability enables faster ramp using existing 200mm fab infrastructure
- Validates Oxford Instruments' ALE technology for production-scale compound semiconductor manufacturing

**Long-term Significance:**
- In-house GaN manufacturing lowers costs and improves yields, accelerating GaN adoption in mainstream power electronics
- EV market benefit: GaN enables smaller, more efficient onboard chargers and DC-DC converters
- AI infrastructure benefit: GaN-based PSUs in data centers offer >99% efficiency, reducing cooling costs
- 200mm GaN production cost parity with Si may be reached sooner than expected

**Key Observation:**
The shift from GaN epitaxial wafers sourced externally to fully in-house manufacturing (epitaxy + etch + metallization) represents a maturing of the GaN ecosystem. ROHM's vertical integration move mirrors trends seen in SiC manufacturing. Watch for other power semiconductor players (Infineon, ST, Nexperia) to follow similar in-house integration strategies.
